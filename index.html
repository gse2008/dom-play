The Wizard of Oz

Lion: You're right, I am a coward. I haven't any courage at all.
I even scare myself. Look at the circles under my eyes. I haven't slept in weeks.

Tin Man: Why don't you try counting sheep?

Lion: That doesn't do any good, I'm afraid of 'em.

Scarecrow: Oh, that's too bad. Don't you think the Wizard could
help him, too?

Dorothy: I don't see why not. Why don't you come along with us?
We're on our way to see the Wizard now. To get him a heart.

Tin Man: And him a brain.

Dorothy: I'm sure he could give you some courage.

Lion: Well, wouldn't you feel degraded to be seen in the company
of a cowardly lion? I would.

Dorothy: No, of course not.
